UNDERSTANDING GOLD TESTING

ENSURING THE PURITY OF YOUR
Precious Investments

by Dhruv Taneja

Explore the dynamic world of gold trading with Srikanth V, Founder and Director of Sristar Gold and Sristar Sona Chandi Limited. As a seasoned expert in the field, Srikanth has a deep understanding of the nuances of gold trading. His insights illuminate the complexity and elegance of this precious metal. In a professional dialogue, he shares valuable knowledge on the importance of gold testing, which is crucial for determining the purity of gold. Pure gold commands a higher value, and ensuring its purity is essential when making a purchase. Srikanth also discusses various methods professionals use to test gold, ensuring its quality and guaranteeing that buyers get what they pay for.

SRIKANTH V

Here are several techniques to verify the purity of gold:

01

The Touch-and-See Method: Fire Testing

Think of this as the gold‚Äôs final exam. The gold is melted down, and the impurities are taken out. What you‚Äôre left with is the amount of real gold. It‚Äôs an old technique but very trustworthy. The downside? The gold has to be melted, so it‚Äôs not the method you‚Äôd use for your favourite ring.

The High-Tech Burn: ICP

Inductively Coupled Plasma (ICP) is a cutting-edge method that vaporizes a sample with a high-temperature plasma torch. By measuring the light emitted at different wavelengths, it can precisely determine gold content, although it requires the destruction of the sample.

The Safe Way: XRF

X-ray fluorescence (XRF) is a modern, non-destructive technique that has gained popularity for its quick and clean analysis. It involves exposing gold to X-rays and analysing the secondary X-ray emissions produced by the different elements in the sample. The benefits of this method include its speed and the fact that it does not damage the gold being tested.

The Quick Check: Scratch Testing & Pens

For those seeking a more immediate and less technical means of testing gold, tough-stone testing involves scratching the gold against a testing stone. By applying acids that react to different purity levels, one can approximate the gold content. Similarly, the gold testing pen is a portable option that uses an electronic tip to estimate the karat rating of gold items.

Each of these testing methods has a purpose. Some are for when you need to know the gold‚Äôs purity down to the last detail, and others are for a quick check on the go. They all play a part in making sure the gold you buy, sell, or wear is just as valuable as it should be.

So, when you‚Äôre looking at gold jewelry or considering a gold investment, remember that these testing methods are what stand behind the gleam, ensuring that your treasure is true. It‚Äôs a mix of tradition and technology working to maintain the trust we place in this precious metal.
